Ode to Underground

A hidden gem, off the beaten track…these are all terms that us travellers seek out with the hope of making a unique discovery. During my last trip to London I decided to forgo searching for 'that tapas joint' or 'that quirky museum' staying ON the beaten track yet without compromising my need to explore the unknown. Steeped in history, London's transport system is one of the most beaten tracks in the world and has always been ahead of its time. Kings Cross/St Pancras was my first stop. Built in 1852 the station recently underwent renovation works and now boasts a futuristic steel and glass lattice gateway to the north without taking from its original features. Going underground I hop on the 'Tube' several changes later and a new found respect for those who built this intricate web of lines, it was time for me to disembark at Covent Garden. If you want to whet your appetite before you decide how many zones you need to travel within, Covent Garden's Transport museum is a must! After a fascinating afternoon I get back on the Tube, arriving at Canary Wharf station to take my first trip on the DLR or Docklands Light Railway. But first I marvel at the sky scraping buildings in London’s financial district. My train arrives taking me to Greenwich in less than 10 minutes. Greenwich is a magical little place evoking its own sense of whimsy at every corner. A stark contrast to the town are the brutalist style dwellings on the outskirts. I sit amidst the Clockwork Orange-esque architecture for a bit taking in the sites. I'm pretty sure I can spot the Shard in the distance as well as the Gherkin and St Paul's Cathedral. I need to be on the other side of the water so I decide to leave my claustrophobic self behind and walk 370 meters under the Thames. Strolling through the Greenwich foot tunnel I marvelled at the 100 year old structure, wondering if I was walking in the footsteps of an Edwardian lady, a common labourer, an artist, a poet…more than likely those scuffed shoe marks were from a fellow tourist but it’s ok to dream. So whether a native or a tourist London's transport system is more than just a way of getting from A to B. Hop on, you wont be disappointed.
